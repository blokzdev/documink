# ADR-021: BIP-39 recovery phrase as a 256-bit entropy codec (not a seed)

- Status: Accepted
- Date: 2026-06-13
- Context: V1 Phase 1d (recovery). Implements blueprint §8.4; relates to ADR-005/ADR-020.

## Context

Blueprint §8.4 requires a BIP-39 recovery phrase, shown once at onboarding and confirmed by
re-entry, that "encodes the Argon2id-derived MK". BIP-39 has two distinct outputs:

1. the **entropy** the words encode (recoverable exactly via `mnemonicToEntropy`), and
2. a **512-bit seed** derived from the words via PBKDF2 (`mnemonicToSeed`) — a one-way expansion.

The Master Key is 256-bit (AES-256 / HKDF-32). To restore the *exact same MK* from a phrase we
must use path (1); the PBKDF2 seed path produces unrelated 512-bit output and could never
reproduce the MK.

## Decision

`RecoveryService` uses BIP-39 purely as a **checksummed 256-bit codec**:
`encodeMasterKey(mk)` = `entropyToMnemonic(hex(mk))` → 24 words; `decodeMnemonic(words)` =
`mnemonicToEntropy(words)` → 32 bytes, after `validateMnemonic` and a 32-byte length check.
256-bit is exactly BIP-39's maximum entropy → 24 words + an 8-bit checksum. `confirms()` compares
the re-entered phrase's MK to the expected MK in constant time. Package: `bip39` (BSD-3-Clause).

The recover→reset-passphrase **orchestration** (use a restored MK to open the vault, then re-key
to a new passphrase) is **out of scope here** and lands with Phase 5 onboarding/UI — 1d delivers
only the codec + validation.

## Rationale

- Exact MK round-trip is mandatory for recovery; only the entropy path provides it.
- The 256-bit ≡ 24-word fit is exact, so no padding/truncation games.
- A checksum gives immediate feedback on mistyped phrases; constant-time confirm avoids leaking how
  much of a re-typed phrase matched.

## Consequences

- `lib/services/recovery_service.dart` is storage-free (never reads/holds the vault); tested
  against the canonical **Trezor BIP-39 256-bit known-answer vectors** plus round-trip,
  subkey-re-derivation, and rejection of bad-checksum / 12-word / non-wordlist phrases.
- **If the MK is ever redefined to be larger than 256 bits, this mapping breaks** and must be
  revisited (STOP-and-surface). The 32-byte assertion in `encodeMasterKey` guards this.
- Like the passphrase, the phrase is never persisted by the app; "lost passphrase + lost phrase =
  permanently inaccessible vault" remains intentional (§8.4).

## Alternatives considered / rejected

- **`mnemonicToSeed` (PBKDF2 seed) path** — standard for HD wallets, but one-way; cannot reproduce
  the MK. Rejected.
- **Custom wordlist/checksum codec** — reinventing BIP-39 with less review. Rejected.
