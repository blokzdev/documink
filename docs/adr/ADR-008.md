# ADR-008: No third-party analytics

- Status: Accepted
- Date: 2026-06-13
- Context: Recorded in V0 Phase 3; formalizes a decision already in the specs.

## Context

DocuMink's core promise is on-device, privacy-first document handling with no mandatory
telemetry. Third-party analytics/ad SDKs would contradict that promise, complicate the Play Data
Safety declaration, and risk exfiltrating sensitive context.

## Decision

Ship **zero third-party analytics and zero ad SDKs**. Forbidden: Firebase Analytics, Mixpanel,
Amplitude, Segment, Google Analytics, all ad networks, tracking-focused push services, and
social-login SDKs beyond Google Drive OAuth (used only for BYOC sync). The only optional
telemetry is opt-in crash reporting (`sentry_flutter`) with aggressive scrubbing. **CI enforces
this**: `tool/scripts/check_analytics_sdks.dart` scans `lib/`, `test/`, `integration_test/`, and
`tool/` and fails the build on any banned import.

## Rationale

- Direct expression of the product principle "no mandatory telemetry" (PRD §2, §4.9).
- A binding privacy invariant, not a preference (`privacy-invariants.md`).
- Enforced mechanically so the guarantee can't silently regress (blueprint §13.3; roadmap V0 P2).

## Consequences

- Product analytics rely on opt-in crash reports only; no behavioral funnels.
- Adding any analytics/ads dependency is a CI failure and a policy violation requiring explicit sign-off.
